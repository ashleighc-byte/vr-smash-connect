School invite

Send this to the four KC Cluster schools to introduce the pilot and confirm their interest. Keep it brief — a fuller requirements checklist follows separately.

ELEVEN — VR Table Tennis Pilot

Kia ora [School Name],

Sport Waikato and Beyond (the team behind GameFIT) are inviting your school to take part in a Waikato-first VR table tennis interschool competition — running during tournament week 31 August – 4 September 2025.

This is a free pilot for KC Cluster schools, delivered using VR headsets that are already in your school through GameFIT. Students compete in Eleven Table Tennis VR — a head-to-head VR game that mirrors real table tennis — against students from the other three schools in the cluster.

What's involved for your school:
  • A staff lead (doesn't have to be a PE teacher)

School requirements checklist

This checklist is for Sport Waikato / Beyond staff to complete in conversation with each school's staff lead. Send to schools alongside the invite.

Safe playing space

  • Clear floor area of at least 2m × 2m per headset (no furniture in swing radius)
  • No fragile objects, glass, or hanging items within the play zone
  • Non-slip flooring or mat available if needed

Tournament rules

Official rules for the Waikato KC Cluster VR Table Tennis Interschool Tournament. These apply to both the school playoffs and tournament week.

Match format

Each match is played as a best of 3 games. Each game is first to 11 points, with a 2-point lead required to win (e.g. 12–10, 13–11). If a game reaches 10–10, play continues until one player leads by 2 (deuce rules). The player who wins 2 games first wins the match.

Scoring system — school points
ResultSchool points awarded
Win a match (2–0)3 pts
Win a match (2–1)2 pts
Lose a match (1–2)1 pt
Lose a match (0–2)0 pts
Walkover win (opponent unable to play)2 pts

Schools are ranked by total points after the round robin. Ties are broken by: (1) head-to-head result, (2) total games won, (3) total points differential.

Player eligibility
  • Players must be currently enrolled students at the school they represent
  • Players must have attended at least one lunchtime practice session before tournament week
  • Each school nominates 4 players as their tournament team, plus 2 reserves
  • Teams must be confirmed with Sport Waikato by Friday 29 August
  • Each player may play a maximum of 2 matches per day during the round robin
  • During finals (Thu–Fri), each school's coach/staff lead selects which players compete
Substitutes
  • Substitutions may be made from the reserve list before a match begins
  • No substitutions mid-match
  • If a named player is absent, a reserve may step in with no penalty to the school
  • If a school cannot field any player, the opposing school receives a walkover (2 points)
  • Schools are encouraged to keep reserves warm — practice sessions during opponent matches are fine

Code of conduct
  • Players must respect the headset and play zone at all times
  • No deliberate physical interference with opponents or spectators
  • Unsportsmanlike behaviour (taunting, abuse) may result in a game forfeit at the referee's discretion
  • Sportsmanship Award nominations are collected throughout the week — this is a real award, not a consolation
  • Beyond/Sport Waikato staff have final say on all rule disputes

Karawhiua house points structure

House points are the engine of participation. Every student can earn points — not just the players who make the tournament team. This structure rewards involvement, effort, and sportsmanship, not just winning.

These points must be logged in the Karawhiua app by the school's staff lead after each session. Confirm app access and logging process with each school before Week 3.
ActivityPointsNotes
Try VR table tennis at lunchtime 5 First time only. Any student, any skill level.
Attend 3 practice sessions 15 Bonus points for commitment. Logged cumulatively.
Enter school playoff 20 All students who sign up for school playoffs, regardless of result.
Represent school in tournament week 50 Named players and reserves who are listed on the team.
Support / spectate during finals 5 All students who attend a finals-day session as supporters.
Sportsmanship award 25 Nominated by peers or staff. One per school per day during tournament week.
Win school playoff 10 Bonus for the student who wins their school's internal playoff.
School wins tournament match 15 All students across the school earn points when their team wins a match during tournament week.
Grand final winner (school) 30 Shared across all students at the winning school.

How to log points in Karawhiua

  • Staff lead opens Karawhiua app after each session
  • Select the relevant activity and the students who participated
  • Points are attributed to each student's house automatically
  • Leaderboard is visible in app — display on school noticeboard weekly
  • If Karawhiua access is not confirmed before Week 3, escalate to Sport Waikato immediately
Don't make it about winning. The points structure is deliberately weighted toward participation, attendance, and sportsmanship — not just making the team or winning matches. A student who tries VR three times and cheers on their school during finals earns 25 points. A player who wins the grand final earns up to 95 points. The gap exists, but participation is meaningfully rewarded.

How to run a lunchtime session

A simple step-by-step guide for school staff leads. Designed for sessions of 30–45 minutes with rotating students. You don't need to be a tech expert — this guide covers everything.

Before students arrive (5 min)

  • Check headsets are charged (aim for 80%+ — plug in overnight before sessions)
  • Clear the play zone: 2m × 2m per headset, no furniture in swing radius
  • Turn on the screen / projector
  • Connect headset to Wi-Fi and start casting to the screen (see troubleshooting guide if needed)
  • Open Eleven VR on each headset — confirm it loads without updates required
  • Set up a rotation list: write student names on a piece of paper, 5-min slots or by game

Opening (3–5 min)

  • Gather students at the screen — don't just hand the headset straight over
  • Briefly explain: "This is Eleven VR — virtual table tennis. You'll take turns. The headset goes on, controllers are paddles. First one to 11 wins a game."
  • Show the play zone boundaries — students MUST stay within the safe zone when wearing the headset
  • Remind students: remove glasses if they have prescription lenses that won't fit, headset accommodates some glasses
  • Let one confident student go first so others can watch on the screen

Running rotations (main session)

  • 5-minute slots work well for "have a go" sessions — enough for 2–3 games
  • Head-to-head sessions (both headsets): one student per headset, playing each other — announce the match on screen
  • AI practice sessions (one headset): student plays against the AI — good for beginners, lets them take their time
  • Call out names clearly when it's the next student's turn — give a 1-min warning
  • Keep the screen casting at all times — this is what keeps watching students engaged
  • Comment on what's happening on screen: "Nice serve!", "Oh close — deuce!" — this builds energy
  • If students are waiting, encourage them to watch the match and predict who'll win

Key safety rules to enforce

  • No-one enters the play zone when a headset is in use
  • Students must stop playing if they feel dizzy or unwell — headset off immediately
  • Swing controllers only in the play zone — other students must stay at least 2m back
  • Remove headset before walking — never walk around in a headset
  • Maximum 20–30 min continuous play — breaks are fine and encouraged

Logging Karawhiua house points

  • Note which students tried VR for the first time today — they earn 5 house points
  • Students attending their 3rd session earn 15 bonus points
  • Log points in Karawhiua app at the end of the session or by end of day
  • Display the leaderboard on screen briefly at the end if possible — house competition drives next session's turnout

Wrapping up (3 min)

  • Give a 2-min warning before the bell
  • Thank students for coming — mention when the next session is
  • Put headsets on charge immediately after the session
  • Wipe headset foam with a lightly damp cloth if many students have used it
  • Note any tech issues for the troubleshooting log — send to Beyond if significant
You don't need to be good at table tennis. Your job is to manage the space, keep students safe, keep the rotation moving, and keep the energy up. Beyond are available by phone/message during sessions if you need real-time tech help.

Troubleshooting guide

Common problems and how to fix them. Keep this open on your phone during sessions. If a problem isn't listed here, call Beyond directly.

Beyond support line during sessions: Jessica Manins — +64 27 514 9599 (text or call)

Headset won't turn on

  • Hold power button for 3 seconds — wait 10 seconds before trying again
  • If still no response: plug into charger for 10 minutes, then try again
  • Check battery level — if under 5%, headset may refuse to boot. Charge first.
  • If headset was left in a hot room/car, let it cool to room temperature before use
  • If still not working: call Beyond

Can't connect to Wi-Fi

  • Check Wi-Fi is on: Settings → Wi-Fi → toggle on if needed
  • If school Wi-Fi requires a web portal login (captive portal), it won't work with Meta Quest — switch to Starlink/hotspot
  • Forget the network and reconnect: Settings → Wi-Fi → tap network → Forget → reconnect
  • Try switching to the 5GHz band if available (listed separately in Wi-Fi list)
  • If firewall is blocking connection: call IT contact. Known fix: whitelist *.oculus.com and *.meta.com

Casting to screen not working

  • Both headset and casting device must be on the same Wi-Fi network
  • To cast: press Meta button → hover over bottom-right corner → select Cast → choose device
  • If casting device not showing: restart the Meta Quest, wait 30 seconds, try again
  • For Chromecast: ensure TV input is set to the correct HDMI port and Chromecast app is open
  • Try a different casting method: cast to phone first, then mirror phone to TV via cable or AirPlay
  • 5GHz band significantly improves casting stability — switch if available
  • If casting fails completely: at minimum, let students watch the player — the match is still on the headset screen visible to nearby students

Multiplayer match won't connect

  • Both players must be signed into different Eleven VR accounts
  • One player creates a private room → shares the room code → other player joins with code
  • If room not found: check both headsets are on the same or a reliable internet connection
  • Restart Eleven VR on both headsets — don't just restart the match, close the app
  • If school internet is blocking: switch both headsets to Starlink/hotspot
  • During tournament week: Beyond will manage all multiplayer connections centrally

Wrong profile / can't log in

  • Each headset should have its own dedicated Meta account — don't share accounts between headsets
  • If a student's practice profile needs resetting: contact Beyond (profile credentials are held by Beyond)
  • For tournament week: player accounts are pre-configured — don't let students change settings or passwords
  • If headset shows a different user: Settings → Accounts → Switch Account

Headset battery low during a session

  • Meta Quest 2 gives approximately 2 hours of play per charge
  • Charge headsets overnight before every session — aim for 100% at the start
  • You can play while charging via a long USB-C cable if needed (play quality is the same)
  • During tournament week: Beyond will bring a charging protocol — headsets rotate between matches

Student feels dizzy or unwell in VR

  • Stop play immediately — have the student remove the headset
  • Sit them down with eyes open, looking at a fixed point in the room
  • VR motion sickness usually passes within 5–10 minutes
  • Don't encourage them to "push through" — VR sickness is real and can worsen
  • Student should not attempt VR again that day
  • Note the student's name and report to Beyond — some students may need shorter initial sessions
  • If symptoms persist beyond 30 min: follow standard school first aid procedure

Eleven VR requires an update

  • Updates are best done the night before a session — plug in headset, connect to Wi-Fi, updates download automatically
  • If an update appears during a session: skip it if possible (you can delay updates in settings)
  • If the update is mandatory and you can't skip: the session may need to run on the other headset only
  • Beyond will check for pending updates as part of their pre-tournament week audit

Privacy & safety guidance

Protecting students is the first priority. These guidelines apply to all schools and all staff involved in the pilot.

No public livestream

The pilot is an internal school event. There will be no public livestream of matches on any platform — not YouTube, not Facebook Live, not TikTok, not Instagram Live.

  • Casting to the in-room screen is for spectators present at the event only
  • If a parent or community member asks about watching online — it is not available for this pilot
  • This protects students who may not be comfortable with wider public visibility
  • Live streaming can be considered for a future iteration once students and schools have opted in explicitly

No student names shared publicly without consent

  • Do not post student names on school social media, Sport Waikato channels, or GameFIT channels during or after the pilot without named consent
  • Tournament results and school standings may be shared without student names (e.g. "Piopio College won the grand final")
  • If a student wants to share their own involvement on personal social media — that is their choice
  • Photo/video of students: only with general school consent OR pilot-specific signed consent
  • Photos showing student faces: do not post without individual consent, even if general school consent exists

Internal school promotion first

  • All promotion during the pilot (posters, notices, Karawhiua) is internal — within the school only
  • Don't cross-promote to other schools' students until the pilot is complete
  • Sport Waikato and Beyond will not tag or share school content without checking with the school lead first
  • If media enquiries come in during the pilot — refer to Leanne Bats at Sport Waikato

Storytelling after the pilot — with approved students only

  • Storytelling (case studies, video, social posts, reports to ITTF or national partners) happens after 4 September, not during
  • Martin and Lisa from Sport Waikato will lead the storytelling process after the pilot
  • Students featured in any story must give explicit consent — and so must their parents/guardians if under 16
  • Students can withdraw consent at any point, even after giving it
  • The story belongs to the students — don't use their voice to promote the programme without their knowledge

Physical safety during VR play

  • Maintain a clear 2m × 2m play zone per headset at all times
  • No other students in the play zone when a headset is in use
  • Stop play immediately if a student feels dizzy, nauseous, or unwell
  • Maximum 20–30 minutes continuous VR play — breaks between rotations are natural
  • All incidents (near-misses, falls, motion sickness) must be logged and reported to Beyond
  • First aid kit must be accessible in the same room during sessions
If you're unsure — ask first. Bill Cooksley (Sport Waikato) or Jessica Manins (Beyond) are the contacts for anything privacy or safety related during the pilot.

A full 6–8 week campaign. Tournament week is the destination — the lead-up is where the real work happens.

Now — this week
Confirm scope and stop scope creep
  • Confirm Waikato-only pilot, 4 KC Cluster schools, ~$14k budget
  • Ask Beyond to revise proposal to cluster model
  • Confirm key roles: Sport Waikato lead, Beyond lead, school staff leads
  • Confirm minimum equipment: 2 headsets / 2 profiles per school (Option B)
  • Confirm tournament week remains 31 Aug – 4 Sep
Week 1 · 1–5 July
Lock the model, prepare school-facing materials
  • Rewrite proposal as Waikato-only cluster model with lead-up and school playoffs
  • Create school invite (ready on this site)
  • Create school requirements checklist (ready on this site)
  • Draft tournament rules and Karawhiua points structure (ready on this site)
  • Confirm with Beyond: headset count, profiles, multiplayer, Eleven licences
Week 2 · 8–12 July
Get schools on board before end of term
  • Send school invite to 4 KC Cluster schools
  • Ask each school to nominate staff lead, confirm space, screen, timing
  • Confirm photo/video consent process at each school
  • Confirm Karawhiua app access for each school
  • Schools receive pilot concept and understand it's more than one week
School holidays · 13–26 July
Build assets and solve tech before students return
  • Beyond/Sport Waikato test: headset setup, profiles, Eleven multiplayer, casting, Starlink/hotspot
  • Create session guide and troubleshooting guide (both ready on this site)
  • Create promo assets: posters, notices, house points explainer, Road to Tournament graphic
  • Create school playoff template and bracket (ready on this site)
  • Create pre-pilot student survey and staff feedback form (ready on this site)
  • Prepare privacy/safety guidance (ready on this site)
Week 3 · 27 Jul – 2 Aug
Campaign launch in schools
  • Launch Karawhiua campaign — open student sign-ups
  • Post school notices and posters
  • Run first lunchtime "have a go" session at each school
  • Test casting to screen at each school
  • Award Karawhiua house points for first participation
  • Start informal school leaderboards
Week 4 · 3–9 Aug
Build skills and confidence
  • Run regular lunchtime practice sessions
  • Run beginner tutorials: serve, scoring, rally, safety, challenging another player
  • Optional "beat a staff member" challenge sessions
  • Continue Karawhiua house competition
  • Confirm school playoff date and tournament week time window
Week 5 · 10–16 Aug
School playoffs
  • Each school runs internal playoffs using bracket on this site
  • Select 4 players + 2 reserves per school
  • Award Karawhiua points for entering, sportsmanship, making team
  • Capture student voice: why they joined, usual activity level
  • Confirm final team list — send to Sport Waikato by 15 August
Week 6 · 17–23 Aug
Full cross-school rehearsal
  • Run a full test match between two schools — profiles, multiplayer, casting, scoring
  • Confirm final draw, daily run sheet, school contacts
  • Confirm prizes: champion school, MVP, sportsmanship, most improved, best supporter house
  • Create finals week hype content
  • No school should test tech for the first time during tournament week
Week 7 · 24–30 Aug
Final readiness
  • Final equipment, profile, casting, and internet checks
  • Publish tournament draw and house points leaderboard
  • Send schools final run sheet — confirm student reps, staff leads, prize pack
  • Run optional open practice sessions
  • Students know who's playing. Supporters know when to watch. Staff know what to do.

Evaluate, tell the story, decide what's next

  • Gather quotes from students and staff
  • Martin & Lisa create pilot story for Sport Waikato
